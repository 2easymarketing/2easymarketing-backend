How to Get Started

Your Step-by-Step Roadmap

Follow this exact path to go from zero experience to a profitable digital marketing business in 12 months.

01

Pick Your Niche

Don't try to serve everyone. Pick one industry you understand — local restaurants, real estate, fitness, e-commerce. Being specific makes you more credible and easier to hire.

Start with:
  • Local service businesses (dentists, lawyers, contractors)
  • E-commerce stores needing more traffic
  • Coaches and consultants building their brand
02

Learn 2 Core Services

Master two services that work well together before adding more. The best beginner combos: SEO + Content, or Paid Ads + Social Media. Depth beats breadth early on.

Best starter pairings:
  • SEO + Content writing
  • Facebook/Instagram Ads + Social Media
  • Email Marketing + CRM setup
03

Get Certified & Build Skills

Free certifications give you credibility and structure your learning. Complete these before approaching clients — they're free and recognized by employers.

Free certifications to get:
  • Google Digital Marketing Certificate
  • Meta Blueprint (Facebook/Instagram Ads)
  • HubSpot Content Marketing Certification
  • Google Analytics 4 Certificate
04

Build Your Brand & Website

Your marketing presence IS your portfolio. Set up a clean website with your services, a contact form, and 2–3 case studies (even if they're for yourself or done as practice work).

Must-haves:
  • Professional website with services listed
  • LinkedIn profile optimized
  • Business email (yourname@yourdomain.com)
  • Portfolio with 2–3 sample projects
05

Set Up Your Tool Stack

You don't need to pay for everything on day one. Start with free tiers, then upgrade as you earn. These tools handle 80% of client work.

Free tools to start with:
  • Google Search Console + GA4 (analytics)
  • Canva (design)
  • Mailchimp free tier (email)
  • Ubersuggest free (SEO research)
  • Buffer free (social scheduling)
06

Land Your First 3 Clients

Your first clients come from your network — not cold ads. Reach out to 50 people you know, offer a discounted first project, and collect a case study in return.

How to approach it:
  • Message ex-colleagues and local business owners
  • Offer 25–40% discount for a 90-day commitment
  • Always request a written testimonial
  • Charge something — free work gets no respect
07

Scale with Systems

Once you have 3 paying clients, build repeatable systems: proposal templates, onboarding checklists, monthly report templates. Systems let you take on more clients without burning out.

What to systematize:
  • Client onboarding flow
  • Monthly reporting templates
  • Content creation workflows
  • Campaign launch checklists
08

Hire & Grow to Agency

By month 6 (MRR $8K–$15K), hire your first contractor to handle execution while you focus on sales and strategy. This is how freelancers become agency owners.

Year-one revenue targets:
  • Months 1–3: $5K–$15K total
  • Months 4–6: $8K–$15K MRR
  • Months 7–9: $15K–$25K MRR
  • Months 10–12: $25K–$40K MRR
